## Twitter/X — 10 Shareable Psychology Insight Posts

**Post 1 — Big Five**
```
Most people know their Big Five scores.

Few know what they mean together.

High openness + low conscientiousness = creative but scattered.
High agreeableness + low extraversion = warm but drained by people.
Low neuroticism + high openness = resilient AND curious — the rarest combo.

Your traits interact. That's where the insight is.
```

**Post 2 — Attachment Style**
```
Your attachment style was formed before you could speak.

Anxious: learned love comes with uncertainty.
Avoidant: learned closeness leads to disappointment.
Disorganized: learned both — simultaneously.
Secure: learned you could count on people.

The good news: it's not fixed. Awareness is the first step to change.
```

**Post 3 — Enneagram**
```
The Enneagram isn't about personality.

It's about your core wound — and the strategy you built to survive it.

Type 2 doesn't just "like helping". They fear being unloved unless useful.
Type 8 doesn't just "like control". They fear being betrayed if they show weakness.

The type is the armor. The wound is the truth.
```

**Post 4 — Schwartz Values**
```
Two people can value "success" and mean completely different things.

Schwartz values theory identifies 10 universal motivators:

Power, Achievement, Hedonism, Stimulation, Self-Direction, Universalism, Benevolence, Tradition, Conformity, Security.

Your hierarchy — not just what you value, but in what order — predicts almost every major life decision.
```

**Post 5 — Jungian Archetypes**
```
Jung's 12 archetypes explain why certain stories feel like they're *about you*.

The Hero needs to prove themselves.
The Caregiver needs to protect others.
The Sage needs to find truth.
The Rebel needs to break rules.

Which archetype is running your life right now?
```

**Post 6 — Growth Tracking**
```
Personality isn't fixed.

I know because I tracked mine.

Took the Big Five twice — once at 24, once at 27.

You're not the same person you were 3 years ago. Your scores shouldn't be either.
```

**Post 7 — Cross-framework synthesis**

```
Here's something most personality tests miss:

An anxious-attached Type 2 Enneagram with high Agreeableness and low Self-Direction values...

...is a completely different person than an anxious-attached Type 4 with high Openness and high Universalism values.

Same attachment pattern. Totally different life. Context is everything.
```

**Type 8 + Disorganized Attachment:** More common than I expected. The 8's need for control as protection and the disorganized pattern of wanting closeness while fearing it seem connected.

**Type 9 + Secure Attachment:** Also surprisingly common. The 9's easy-going nature may develop from (or reinforce) secure early attachment patterns.

Has anyone else noticed these correlations? I'm curious whether they match your self-understanding.

## IndieHackers Post

**Title:** How we synthesized 5 psychology frameworks into one AI-generated portrait (and what we learned about personality tech)

**Body:**

I've been building in the psychology/self-knowledge space for the past year. Today I'm launching Innermind publicly and wanted to share what we learned.

**The problem we're solving**

The personality assessment market is fragmented. MBTI has 500M tests taken. Big Five is the scientific standard. Enneagram has a devoted community. Attachment theory is having a cultural moment. But each platform gives you one lens — and they don't talk to each other.

The insight that started this: a person with anxious attachment, Type 2 Enneagram, high Agreeableness, and Caregiver archetype is describing the same underlying pattern from four different angles. What if we synthesized all of them?

**What we built**

Innermind runs 5 validated frameworks (Big Five, Schwartz Values, Attachment Style, Enneagram, Jungian Archetypes) and then uses Claude to generate a unified psychological portrait. Not a report. A narrative that weaves the frameworks into a coherent picture of that specific person.

Beyond the portrait:
- Growth tracking: retake assessments and see how scores change over time
- Adaptive deep-dive: AI generates personalized follow-up questions based on your results
- Reflection journal: link insights to real life events
- AI coach: conversational coaching grounded in your full profile

**Tech stack**

Next.js 14 + Fastify API + PostgreSQL/Prisma + Claude for AI features + Stripe + Vercel/Railway

**Early numbers**

Still very early. Launched to a small beta. Retention has been better than expected — people keep coming back to retake assessments after significant life events.

**Lessons learned**

1. The synthesis is the product. Individual framework scores are table stakes. The value is the cross-framework narrative.
2. Validated instruments matter. We spent weeks getting the assessment questions right. Users notice when they feel "off".
3. The coach is the stickiest feature. Everything else drives acquisition; the coach drives retention.

Try it at innermind.app. Happy to answer questions — especially from anyone who's built in the self-knowledge/psychology space.
