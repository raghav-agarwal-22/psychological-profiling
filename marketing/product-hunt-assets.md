## ✅ Product Description (PH listing body)

**Headline:** Innermind — Know yourself through five psychology frameworks

**Body:**
Most self-help apps give you one personality type and call it done. Innermind runs five validated psychology frameworks — Big Five, Schwartz Values, Attachment Style, Enneagram, and Jungian Archetypes — then synthesizes them into a single, nuanced psychological portrait.

**What makes Innermind different:**
- **Five frameworks, one portrait** — your Big Five conscientiousness, your Enneagram type, and your attachment pattern all say something different. Innermind shows how they connect.
- **Growth tracking** — retake any assessment and see how you have changed over time with a score delta chart
- **Adaptive deep-dive** — Claude analyzes your results and generates 10 personalized follow-up questions for a portrait unique to you
- **Reflection journal** — capture insights as they happen, linked to your assessments
- **AI coach** — conversational coaching grounded in your actual psychological profile, not generic advice

Built for people doing serious inner work: therapists' clients, coaches, founders, and anyone who wants to understand why they do what they do.

## ✅ Maker First Comment

> Hi PH community! I built Innermind because I was tired of personality tests that tell you what *type* you are without telling you *why* you are that way.
>
> After taking the Big Five for the tenth time, I realized the real insight isn't in any single framework — it's in how all your traits *interact*. Your attachment style shapes how your values play out. Your Enneagram type colors your archetypal patterns. None of these tests tell you that alone.
>
> So I built a tool that takes all five validated frameworks and asks Claude to synthesize them into one coherent portrait — written in plain language, grounded in research, and genuinely personal.
>
> The adaptive deep-dive is my favorite feature: after you complete an assessment, Claude reads your exact scores and generates 10 follow-up questions specifically for *your* profile. Nobody gets the same questions.
>
> Would love your thoughts — especially from anyone who has been on a self-understanding journey and felt like the tools weren't keeping up.
