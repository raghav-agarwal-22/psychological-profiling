---

Hey Product Hunt 👋

I built Innermind because I was frustrated with personality tests that gave me a label and left me with nothing to do with it.

I've taken the Big Five, the MBTI, the Enneagram, and more — and they all said something true, but something incomplete. The real picture only emerged when I looked at all of them together: *how* I process the world (Big Five), *why* I do what I do (Schwartz Values), *who* I unconsciously am (Jungian Archetypes), *how* I connect with others (Attachment Style), and *what core fear drives me* (Enneagram).

Innermind synthesizes all five into one AI-generated portrait. Not a score dump — a narrative. Then it lets you track how you change over time, journal against your insights, and chat with an AI coach that has genuinely read your psychology.

The people I've seen get the most from it: therapists using it with clients before sessions, coaches who want a baseline before engagement, and individuals going through major life transitions who want a real mirror.

It's free to start and takes 8–15 minutes. Would love to hear which archetype you get 🙏

— [Founder Name]

**Edit:** A few folks asked — yes, we use Claude (Anthropic) for both the synthesis and the AI coach. The synthesis prompt cross-references all five frameworks and looks for contradictions, not just strengths. That's where the real insight lives.

## 8. FAQ — Answers for Expected PH Comments

Prepare these in advance so anyone on the team can respond quickly and consistently.

---

**Q: How is this different from 16Personalities / MBTI?**
> Innermind uses five scientifically validated frameworks (not MBTI, which has low test-retest reliability). More importantly, it synthesizes them: contradictions across frameworks are often where the deepest insight lives. We also track how you change over time, which no other tool does.

**Q: Is the AI actually accurate or is it just hallucinating?**
> Great question. The AI synthesis is grounded entirely in your scored responses — it doesn't guess or extrapolate beyond the data. We use Claude (Anthropic) with a structured prompt that cross-references all five frameworks and flags tensions. You'll see citations to your actual scores throughout.

**Q: Is my data private? Who can see my profile?**
> Your profile is private by default. You control sharing — you can share a public link or keep it completely private. We don't sell or share your psychological data with third parties. See our privacy policy for full details.

**Q: How long does it take?**
> The full five-assessment battery takes 8–15 minutes. You can also take assessments one at a time and return for others later.

**Q: Is it free?**
> Free to start — you can take all five assessments and see your basic profile. The AI-synthesized portrait, growth tracking, AI coach, and reflection journal are on our Pro tier ($9/mo or $79/yr). Coaches and therapists interested in using it with clients should check out innermindhealing.com/for-coaches.

**Q: What frameworks do you use and why these five?**
> Big Five (personality dimensions), Schwartz Values (what motivates you), Attachment Style (how you connect), Enneagram (core fear/desire), and Jungian Archetypes (unconscious identity). We chose these because they have the strongest empirical support, they're non-redundant (each measures something different), and together they give a 360° view of personality.

**Q: Can I use this with my therapy clients?**
> Yes — we specifically designed the platform for practitioners. Check out our early advocate program at innermindhealing.com/for-coaches. We offer discounted group access and a shared dashboard (in beta).

**Q: Will you add more frameworks?**
> We're considering IQ-adjacent cognitive assessments and attachment-to-work instruments. We'd rather do five frameworks deeply than ten frameworks shallowly.

**Q: Does this replace therapy?**
> No — and we're explicit about that. Innermind is a self-knowledge tool, not a clinical instrument. Many therapists use it as a starting point because it saves 2–3 sessions of baseline exploration. But it's not a substitute for professional support.

**Q: Is there a mobile app?**
> The web app is fully responsive and works great on mobile. A native app is on the roadmap.
