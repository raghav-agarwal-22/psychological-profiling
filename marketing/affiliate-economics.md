## 1. Commission Rate Recommendation: **30% Recurring**

### Rate Comparison

| Rate | Monthly per Sub | Annual per Sub | Gross Margin Retained | Verdict |
|------|----------------|----------------|----------------------|---------|
| 20%  | $3.80          | $29.80         | 80%                  | Below market — hard to recruit top creators |
| **30%** | **$5.70**   | **$44.70**     | **70%**              | ✅ **Recommended — competitive and defensible** |
| 40%  | $7.60          | $59.60         | 60%                  | Attracts large affiliates but compresses margins |

**Verdict: 30% recurring is the right rate.**

- Industry standard for SaaS affiliate programs: 20–40% recurring
- 30% is compelling enough to win creator mindshare vs. alternatives
- Leaves 70% gross margin on every affiliate-referred subscriber — sustainable at scale
- Raising to 40% only justified for mega-affiliates (>1M audience) as a negotiated VIP tier

## 3. Break-Even Analysis: Affiliate vs. Paid Acquisition

### CAC Comparison by Channel

| Channel | Upfront CAC | Ongoing Cost | Effective Cost / Subscriber LTV |
|---------|-------------|--------------|--------------------------------|
| Google Ads | ~$800–$1,200 per Pro conversion | None after acquisition | $800–$1,200 one-time |
| Meta/Facebook Ads | ~$600–$1,000 per Pro conversion | None after acquisition | $600–$1,000 one-time |
| Organic SEO | ~$0 marginal | 0 | ~$0 (but 6–12 month ramp) |
| **Affiliate (30% recurring)** | **$0 upfront** | **$5.70/mo per active sub** | **~$114 over 20-month avg LTV** |

*LTV calculation: $19/mo ÷ 5% monthly churn = $380 average LTV. Affiliate earns 30% = $114 total over subscriber lifetime.*

### Break-Even Finding

**Affiliate is cheaper than paid acquisition at every volume.**

- Paid ads: Pay $800–$1,200 upfront, keep 100% of revenue forever
- Affiliate: Pay $0 upfront, pay $114 over ~20 months (30% cut)
- **Net LTV advantage: affiliate saves $686–$1,086 per subscriber vs. paid ads in upfront cost**
- Trade-off: affiliate has no volume ceiling (you recruit more affiliates, you get more subscribers); paid ads scale with budget

## 4. Cookie Duration Recommendation: **90 Days**

| Duration | Capture Rate | Verdict |
|----------|-------------|---------|
| 30 days | Misses ~40% of conversion decisions | ❌ Too short for self-discovery products |
| 60 days | Captures most consideration cycles | ⚠️ Acceptable but suboptimal |
| **90 days** | **Captures full consideration cycle** | ✅ **Recommended** |

**Rationale:** Psychology and self-discovery products have long consideration cycles. A user who clicks an affiliate link in a YouTube video may:
- Research the product for 1–4 weeks
- Come back after seeing a second mention
- Take the free tier and convert weeks later

90-day cookies are standard for high-consideration SaaS. Competitors (BetterHelp, Noom) use 30–45 days; 90 days is a competitive advantage for affiliate recruitment.
