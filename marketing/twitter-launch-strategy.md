## Engagement Playbook

### Target Accounts to Engage With Daily (20–30 min/day)

Engage by replying with genuine insights — not promotional content. Build reputation before broadcasting.

**Psychology educators & researchers:**
- @adamgrant (Wharton, organizational psychology — huge audience)
- @DrNickWignall (clinical psychologist, emotion regulation content)
- @PsychologyToday (repost relevant studies)
- @YaleCourses (Laurie Santos / happiness research)
- @BreneBrown (vulnerability + shame research — Enneagram-adjacent)

**Personality/MBTI community:**

- @TypesinMind (MBTI educator)

**Self-improvement / productivity:**
- @JamesClear (Atomic Habits — Conscientiousness angle)
- @hubermanlab (neuroscience + psychology crossover)
- @andrewdepolo (growth content)
- @waitbutwhy (Tim Urban — long-form thinker, psychology-adjacent)

**Mental health / therapy:**
- @therapyjeff (therapist content creator)
- @the.holistic.psychologist (attachment, inner child)
- @nedratawwab (attachment theory, boundaries)

### Daily Engagement Protocol (20–30 min)

**Morning (10 min):**
- Reply to 3–5 tweets from the target accounts above
- Add genuine value: extend their point, cite a related research finding, share a relevant framework distinction
- Never mention Innermind unless directly relevant

**Evening (10–15 min):**
- Search hashtags: `#psychology`, `#MBTI`, `#personalitytypes`, `#attachmentstyle`, `#enneagram`
- Reply to high-engagement posts (50+ likes) with valuable additions
- Follow anyone who engages substantively with your posts

### Engagement Rules
- **Never post promotional content as a reply** — builds resentment, gets muted
- **The "1% rule":** For every 100 impressions you want, you need to give 1 substantive engagement first
- **Quote-tweet > Retweet** — adds your voice, gets more engagement
- **Reply to everyone who replies to you** — especially in the first hour after posting

## Content Amplification

### When a Post Gets Traction (50+ likes within 4h)
1. **Quote-tweet it with a follow-on question** — keeps engagement going, new algorithm surface
2. **Cross-post the link to relevant subreddits** (not the full content — "Wrote this thread on X, curious what this community thinks: [link]")
3. **Add it to a curated "thread" in your profile highlights**

### Thread Formatting Best Practices
- Hook tweet must create a knowledge gap in < 280 characters
- Every tweet should end with either a forward pull or a complete thought (not a dangler)
- Use plain text + minimal emoji — psychology audience skews toward substantive, not performative
- Include 1 concrete data point per thread (r-values, study citations) — builds credibility
- Final tweet is always a soft CTA + link
