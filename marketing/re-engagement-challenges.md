# Re-Engagement: Weekly Growth Challenge System

**Task:** INN-130 | **Owner:** CMO
**Status:** Copy complete — Engineering implementation pending

---

## Overview

Weekly "growth challenge" emails for free users who completed an assessment but went inactive (14+ days, no Pro). Each email surfaces one personalized challenge tailored to the user's primary Jungian archetype, targeting their **growth edges** (not their strengths). A teaser of locked Pro features anchors a soft upgrade CTA.

---

## Segmentation Rules

| Segment | Criteria | Email |
|---|---|---|
| **Re-engagement** | Signed up 7+ days ago, completed ≥1 assessment, last login >14 days ago, never Pro | Weekly Growth Challenge (this sequence) |
| **Active Free** | Last login ≤14 days ago, not Pro | Skip — don't interrupt; they're engaged |
| **Onboarding** | Signed up <7 days ago | Onboarding drip (Day1–Day7) takes priority |
| **Pro** | Any tier ≥ Essential | Skip — already converted |

**Archetype required:** User must have completed the Jungian Archetypes assessment. If not, fall back to Big Five dominant trait until archetype is available.

**Cadence:** Weekly, Friday morning send (highest open rates for self-reflection content). Cap at 8 weeks before suppressing.

---

## 8 Challenge Themes

These eight universal themes map to common growth edges across the 12 archetypes. Each archetype gets 4 challenges drawn from the themes that target *their* specific underdeveloped territory.

| # | Theme | Core Invitation |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Courage & Action** | Step into discomfort; do the thing you've been delaying |
| 2 | **Self-Awareness** | Honest self-examination; question your assumptions |
| 3 | **Relationships** | How you show up for others; intimacy and connection |
| 4 | **Boundaries** | Saying no; claiming space; protecting your energy |
| 5 | **Creativity & Expression** | Make something; bring it into the world |
| 6 | **Vulnerability** | Let yourself be seen; ask for help |
| 7 | **Rest & Recovery** | Sustainable wellbeing; not grinding |
| 8 | **Purpose & Meaning** | The "why" behind your doing |

## 4 Growth Challenges Per Archetype

### The Hero
*Strength: achievement, action. Growth edge: receiving help, vulnerability, rest, imperfection.*

1. **(Vulnerability)** Share one struggle you're currently facing with someone who cares about you — without framing it as already solved or on its way to being fixed. Just let it be hard for a moment.
2. **(Relationships)** Let someone help you with something you'd normally handle alone. Notice what arises when you practice receiving instead of doing.
3. **(Rest & Recovery)** Take one full day this week with no achievement agenda. No goals, no output, no progress. Observe what surfaces when you stop.
4. **(Self-Awareness)** Write about a moment you failed at something that mattered — and what you learned about yourself that success couldn't have taught you.

---

### The Sage
*Strength: wisdom, analysis, truth-seeking. Growth edge: action, emotional presence, not knowing.*

1. **(Courage & Action)** Identify one insight you've been sitting with but not acting on. Take one concrete, imperfect step toward it this week. No more thinking first.
2. **(Vulnerability)** Share a time you were genuinely wrong about something important — out loud, to someone you respect. No hedging. Just "I was wrong."
3. **(Relationships)** Have a conversation where your only goal is to listen and understand. No teaching, no correcting, no adding your perspective. Just curious presence.
4. **(Self-Awareness)** Write down three beliefs you hold most strongly. Then spend ten minutes arguing the opposite of each — as compellingly as you can.

---

### The Explorer
*Strength: freedom, curiosity, discovery. Growth edge: commitment, depth, belonging, confronting avoidance.*

1. **(Boundaries)** Commit to one thing for an entire week without switching, pivoting, or exploring something new — even when restlessness and curiosity pull you elsewhere. Finish what you started.
2. **(Relationships)** Choose depth over breadth: have one long, unhurried conversation with someone you care about. Nowhere to be, no agenda. Just be with them.
3. **(Self-Awareness)** Journal on this prompt: *"What am I running from by always moving toward the next horizon?"* Be honest with yourself.
4. **(Purpose & Meaning)** Identify one place in your life where your love of freedom has become a way to avoid something. Name it clearly.

---

### The Creator
*Strength: innovation, vision, making new things. Growth edge: finishing, collaboration, perfectionism, rest.*

1. **(Courage & Action)** Finish something you've started and release it — ship it, share it, or show it to someone, even if it isn't perfect yet. Done is better than ideal.
2. **(Relationships)** Collaborate on something creative with another person. Let their ideas genuinely shape the work alongside yours, without redirecting or taking over.
3. **(Self-Awareness)** Notice where your perfectionism is protecting you from criticism rather than serving the work. Write honestly about what you're actually afraid will happen if you release it.
4. **(Rest & Recovery)** Take one creative sabbath — no making, no producing, no improving. Just consume, wander, and receive. Let the well refill.

---

### The Ruler
*Strength: leadership, responsibility, order. Growth edge: control, vulnerability, delegating, asking for help.*

1. **(Vulnerability)** Identify one area where you're holding on too tightly. Release control in that area for 24 hours — don't check in, don't adjust. Observe what happens.
2. **(Relationships)** Ask for genuine input from someone you'd normally just inform. Actually incorporate what they say, even if it's not what you'd have chosen.
3. **(Self-Awareness)** Write honestly about the cost your standards and expectations have had on your closest relationships this year. Be specific.
4. **(Rest & Recovery)** Delegate something meaningful this week instead of doing it yourself. Practice trusting others to handle what matters.

---

### The Caregiver
*Strength: compassion, nurturing, service. Growth edge: self-care, boundaries, receiving, giving from depletion.*

1. **(Boundaries)** Say no to one request this week that you'd normally accept out of guilt, habit, or fear of disappointing someone. Just: "I can't do that right now."
2. **(Self-Awareness)** Journal on this prompt: *"What do I need that I'm not giving myself?"* Answer as carefully as you would if a loved one asked you the question about them.
3. **(Vulnerability)** Let someone take care of you this week — accept help without minimizing it, redirecting it back to them, or immediately finding a way to reciprocate. Just receive it.
4. **(Purpose & Meaning)** Identify one way you've been giving from depletion rather than fullness. What would you need to change to give from a genuinely full place?

---

### The Magician
*Strength: transformation, catalyzing change, alchemy. Growth edge: being known, taking action vs. visioning, over-helping.*

1. **(Courage & Action)** Choose one transformation you've been envisioning but not initiating. Take the very first concrete step this week — not a plan, not a vision board. An action.
2. **(Relationships)** Help one person see themselves differently this week — reflect something true and beautiful they may not see in themselves. Be a mirror, not a fixer.
3. **(Self-Awareness)** Notice where your insight into others becomes a way to avoid being deeply known yourself. What do you deflect with wisdom or perspective?
4. **(Boundaries)** Identify one situation where you're trying to transform or fix someone who hasn't asked for your help. Practice releasing it this week.

---

### The Lover
*Strength: passion, intimacy, beauty. Growth edge: losing self in others, wanting without asking, full presence.*

1. **(Boundaries)** Identify one relationship where you've lost yourself — your preferences, your needs — in trying to connect or please. Reclaim one clear preference this week and express it.
2. **(Self-Awareness)** Journal on this prompt: *"What do I want that I'm afraid to want fully?"* Don't edit the answer.
3. **(Courage & Action)** Do something purely for beauty this week — not for anyone else's benefit, not as a gift, not to share. Just for the sake of what moves you.
4. **(Relationships)** Have one conversation where you are fully present — phone away, no planning your next words, nowhere else to be. Just here, just this person.

---

### The Jester
*Strength: joy, humor, spontaneity, play. Growth edge: depth, earnestness, facing hard things without deflecting.*

1. **(Purpose & Meaning)** Notice where your humor has become armor. Where does lightness protect you from depth, from difficulty, or from being genuinely seen by someone?
2. **(Courage & Action)** Commit to something serious and consistent for one week — something that requires showing up even when it isn't fun, interesting, or funny.
3. **(Relationships)** Have one earnest, undefended conversation with someone you care about — and when it gets uncomfortable, stay with it instead of making a joke.
4. **(Self-Awareness)** Journal on this prompt: *"What would I be afraid of if I stopped making people laugh?"* Take your time with the answer.

---

### The Orphan
*Strength: empathy, realism, common humanity. Growth edge: learned helplessness, trust, belonging, allowing hope.*

1. **(Courage & Action)** Identify something important you've given up on because it seemed too risky or unlikely. Take one small, specific step back toward it this week.
2. **(Relationships)** Trust one person with something you've been protecting. Practice being genuinely seen — without managing how they respond or pre-explaining yourself.
3. **(Self-Awareness)** Notice the difference between healthy realism and the part of you that expects to be let down. Which voice has been louder this week?
4. **(Purpose & Meaning)** Find one community, group, or cause where you genuinely belong. Show up for it this week — not to observe, but to participate.

---

### The Warrior
*Strength: discipline, mastery, honor, principled action. Growth edge: winning vs. connecting, rest, following, surrender.*

1. **(Vulnerability)** Identify one area where you've prioritized winning or being right over genuine connection. Choose connection this week — even if it means conceding the point.
2. **(Rest & Recovery)** Practice disciplined rest: schedule recovery the way you'd schedule training, and honor it with the same commitment and non-negotiability.
3. **(Relationships)** Let someone else lead something you care about this week. Practice supporting rather than directing — and notice what becomes possible.
4. **(Self-Awareness)** Journal on this prompt: *"What am I fighting for that no longer deserves the battle?"* Be ruthlessly honest.

---

### The Innocent
*Strength: optimism, trust, renewal, faith. Growth edge: naivety, avoiding hard truths, courage in uncertainty.*

1. **(Courage & Action)** Do one thing this week that you know is right even though it might not end well. Choose integrity over guaranteed safety.
2. **(Self-Awareness)** Notice where your optimism is actually avoidance. What are you hoping will "just work out" that actually needs your honest attention and intervention?
3. **(Boundaries)** Identify one situation where you've been naive about someone's intentions or the true state of things. What would clear-eyed trust look like — trust with open eyes?
4. **(Relationships)** Have one honest conversation about something you've been keeping positive on the surface. Say the true thing, even if it isn't the comfortable one.

## Engineering Handoff Notes

- **Challenge selection:** Look up user's `primaryArchetype` from their Jungian narrative. Rotate through 4 challenges sequentially (week 1 → challenge 1, week 2 → challenge 2, etc.), then repeat.
- **Fallback:** If no Jungian assessment completed, use a generic challenge from the "Self-Awareness" theme and include a soft prompt to complete the archetype assessment.
- **UTM params:** Append `?utm_source=email&utm_medium=reengagement&utm_campaign=growth_challenge&utm_content={archetype}` to all profile links.
- **Suppression:** Stop after 8 consecutive sends with no click, or on Pro conversion, or on unsubscribe.
- **Send time:** Friday, 8am user local time (or 9am ET if timezone unknown).
